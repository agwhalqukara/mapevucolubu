    Early Life

    Andrew Joseph “Andy” Cohen was born in St. Louis, Missouri, on June 2, 1968, to his parents Lou Cohen and Evelyn Cohen. He was born into a Jewish family.

    Career

    Andy Cohen spent ten years with CBS News and later on went on to become the producer of the shows ‘CBS This Morning’ and ’48 Hours’. He was also the senior producer of the show called ‘The Early Show.’ In 2000, he became the vice president of Trio, a cable network. In 2004, the network Trio was bought by Bravo, and subsequently, Cohen went on to join Bravo.

    He has co-hosted many shows like ‘The View’ and ‘Live! With Kelly’, and has been regular in the shows ‘Morning Joe’ and ‘Today.’ He has also made several guest appearances on various TV shows such as ‘Saturday Night Live’ and ‘The Comeback.’

    In 2011 and 2012, he hosted both the Miss USA and Miss Universe pageants. He has also hosted NBC’s ‘Hollywood Game Night’ in 2015.

    Cohen appeared in the music video “G.U.Y” by Lady Gaga in 2014.

    He has his own radio channel called Radio Andy on SiriusXM. It features several personalities like Jim Parsons, Jonathan Alter, Dan Rather, Sandra Bernhard, Jill Kargman, John Benjamin Hickey, and himself. He also appears on Channel 102 on SiriusXM.

    In 2017, Cohen began hosting the reboot of ‘Love Connection’ on Fox.

    Awards & Achievements

    As an Executive Producer, Andy Cohen has won the 2010 Emmy Award for ‘Outstanding Competitive Reality Series.’ He has also received the Peabody Award in 2004 and 2007 for the documentary ‘The N-Word’ and TV series ‘Project Runway,’ respectively.
