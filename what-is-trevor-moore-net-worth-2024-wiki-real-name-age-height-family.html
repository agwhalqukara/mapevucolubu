    Trevor Moore was a beacon of comedy, lighting up the industry with his unique blend of humor, intelligence, and creativity.

    Growing up, Trevor was constantly on the move due to his parents’ music career, which meant new schools and new faces.

    Education: Shaping the Future Star

    FAQs about Trevor Moore

    Who was Trevor Moore?

    He was an American comedian, actor, writer, and musician. He was best known as a co-founder of the sketch comedy group The Whitest Kids U Know.

    Did he release any comedy albums?

    Yes, he released three comedy albums: Drunk Texts to Myself (2013), High in Church (2015), and The Story of Our Times (2018), all with Comedy Central Records.

    What television shows did Moore create or work on?

    Moore created and worked on multiple shows, including the Disney Channel show Just Roll With It and The Trevor Moore Show on Comedy Central.

    When and how did Trevor Moore die?

    He died on August 7, 2021, at the age of 41, due to an accident at his residence that resulted in blunt force head trauma. He reportedly fell from the balcony of his home.

    What was his early career like?

    Moore became the world’s youngest published cartoonist at 15 with his book Scraps and created comic strips for Virginia newspapers. He also had a local TV sketch comedy program, The Trevor Moore Show.

    How did The Whitest Kids U Know start?

    The comedy troupe began when Moore teamed up with Zach Cregger, Sam Brown, Timmy Williams, and Darren Trumeter in New York. They gained national attention after winning Best Sketch Group at the HBO US Comedy Arts Festival in 2006.

    What was Moore’s personal life like?

    Moore married Aimee Carlson in October 2010, and they had one son together.
