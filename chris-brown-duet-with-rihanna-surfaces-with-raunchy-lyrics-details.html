    Too much? A collaboration between Rihanna and her ex-boyfriend Chris Brown surfaced on Monday, April 20, and the track features some seriously raunchy lyrics. 

    The estranged lovers, who had an infamously combative relationship and multiple ugly breakups, reportedly recorded the song more than a year ago for potential inclusion on Brown's 2014 album, x. While it never made it onto the record, the track, called "Put It Up," has finally seen the light of day.

    Over a mid-tempo R&B beat, Rihanna, now 27, croons, "Baby you don't need to be scared of me / I guarantee that I'm not a mistake / I'mma give you all of my love / And I hope that you won't misplace it / You created this s–t / Going insane with all of this fear / Can't turn my back on it / I'm in love gotta' face it." 

    The "American Oxygen" singer broke up with Brown, now 25, in 2009, following his brutal assault that left her unable to perform at the Grammy Awards the day after the domestic violence. The two later reconciled in January 2013, and Rih said of Brown at the time, "Even if it's a mistake, it's my mistake. After being tormented for so many years, being angry and dark, I'd rather just live my truth and take the backlash. I can handle it."
